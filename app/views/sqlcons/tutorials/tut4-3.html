Ch4-3 : Having

Now that we have the basics of grouping down, let's move on to the having clause.

This clause acts almost as a "where" clause for aggregate function columns. For instance: Let's say we wanted to collect a count of students classes in the course_fct table, but only where that student had more than 2 classes. We can't just do where count(*) > 1 because that column doesn't exist in the database. Instead we use having like below
select count(*), student_id from course_fcts group by student_id having count(*) > 1
